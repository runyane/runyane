Experiences

Supporting Students

Club Sponsor

Growth in middle school comes in many disquises. Early in my career I had the opportunity to sponsor the Gaming Club at my school Students from all grade levels would come together to share their favorite games and learn about the gaming industry as a whole. I introduced young minds to a variety of board games, card games, and table top games. My students learned to work together to achieve goals and build projects. Weekly we came together to play games and share stories about game related things that inspired them to achieve more.

As the club sponsor, I organized regular tournaments for students to showcase their gaming skills. Class console games became the focus and students battled it out in as a variety of avatars. Board games where players identify a villian or capture a rogue also took center stage. Students relished in the ability to over take their classmates and discover the spy.

Working Educators

Dynamics

One experience that I, and many of my colleagues, find challenging is maintianing order throughout the day. While many think of classroom management as an internal process, it really extends to any moment of the day. Being able to understand the needs of each students during the day can be challeneging. Even more so if that student is strugglingg that day as well. One thing I do is give teachers strategies to deal with the everyday. As a teacher leader, I help teachers retain order not just in their classrooms, but throughout the building as well.

Projects

Teacher Leader

Another aspect of being an educator that I take immense pride in is helping other teachers hone their practice. As a Teacher Leader I mentor teachers through various aspects of instruction. Together we focus on instructional practices that foster inqury and collaboration. We discuss challeneges and strengths; determining areas of improvement and identying aspects of current lessons that worked well was cruical. Multiple sessions of observation coupled with regular debriefings help veteran and novice teachers continue to support students in their academics. Together we worked to craft effective strategies to foster learning in the classroom.

Capstone Project

For a variety of reasons, last summer I decided to try out Udemy's Software Engineering course. In one of its capstone project, this course asked students to code a food blog website using the many languages that were highlighted throughout the course. For me, this course was my first real dive into javascript. Some of my favorite aspects were content and style. The modular aspects of the blog made it easy to be flexable with the tools. Choosing the recipes, adding the layout, and styling each section was challenging. I wrestled with adding functionality. In the end, the project turned out better than expected and I enjoyed the challenge.

Curriculum Mentor

This experience took me out of my comfort zone where I helped to implement the adoption of a new curriculum throughout the district. Each year my districts teachers come together for a few days of collaborative learning on new practices in the field. As a member of the facilitation team, I worked to educate educators on the ins and outs of our new curriculum. We were able to provide detailed guidance where to find lesson plans, where standards are located, or how to approach specific concepts or sections. I enjoyed challenging teachers to add to their practice in a variety of ways.
